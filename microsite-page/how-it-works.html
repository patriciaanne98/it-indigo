How it works (no fluff)

Social platforms make money when you stay longer. So they build systems that predict what will keep you engaged, then rank your feed around it.

See the loop demo

The 3-step pipeline

  1. Capture attention (design + notifications)
  2. Learn behavior (signals + prediction)
  3. Monetize (ads + targeting)

Step 1: Engagement = money

If a platform earns revenue through advertising, every extra minute you stay creates more chances to show ads. That’s why “time spent” becomes a core success metric.

Translation: You’re not paying with dollars — you’re paying with time and attention.

Step 2: AI ranks your feed

Most feeds aren’t chronological. They’re curated. The platform predicts what you’ll click, watch, or react to, then prioritizes it.

Signals include likes, comments, watches, pauses, shares, and what you linger on.

Step 3: The loop (why it feels addictive)

The strongest habit loop isn’t constant rewards — it’s unpredictable rewards. That “maybe there’s something new” feeling keeps you checking.

Examples: pull-to-refresh, infinite scroll, notifications, “suggested posts,” autoplay.

Humans as product

In ad-based platforms, the user isn’t the customer — advertisers are. Your behavior becomes data that helps decide which ads to show and what content to push.

Hard truth: If the platform is free, the “product” is usually your attention + behavioral data.
