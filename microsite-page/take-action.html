Practical • Not preachy

TAKE YOUR ATTENTION BACK

You don’t have to delete everything. The goal is to add friction — small barriers that stop the automatic scroll. Think: make the “pull” less effortless.

3-step starter plan Build boundaries

Starter plan (10 minutes)

Do this once. Feel the difference today.

1) Kill “non-human” notifications

Turn off likes, follows, suggested posts, streaks, “someone posted,” etc. Keep only messages from real people you actually know.

2) Add a timer before you open

Set a 10-minute limit before you open the app. Not after. You’re training intention: “I’m opening this for X reason.”

3) Move the apps off your home screen

Put social apps on the last page or inside a folder. Tiny inconvenience = huge behavior change.

Boundaries that actually work

Pick 2. Make them your rules for a week.

Replace the cue (don’t fight it)

  • If you scroll when bored → open Notes and write 2 sentences instead.
  • If you scroll while eating → put your phone on the counter.
  • If you scroll before bed → charge your phone across the room.
Rule: don’t rely on willpower — change the environment.

Use “one purpose” opens

Before opening, say the purpose out loud:

  • “I’m checking messages.”
  • “I’m posting something.”
  • “I’m looking up one thing.”

If you can’t name the purpose, don’t open the app.

Reflection (30 seconds)

This is how you “wake up” mid-scroll.

Ask 3 questions

  1. What did I come here for?
  2. Did I get it?
  3. What do I actually want to do next?

Mini goal

Reduce scroll time by 15% this week — not 100%. Sustainable change beats dramatic deletion.
